Introduction to Machine Learning

Featured Books

  • Understanding Machine Learning: From Theory to Algorithms

  • Authors:

    Shai Shalev-Shwartz, Shai Ben-David

  • Publishers:

    Cambridge University Press
  • 449 pages
  • (278)
  • The book provides an extensive theoretical account of the fundamental ideas underlying machine learning and the mathematical derivations that transform these principles into practical algorithms.
  • Algorithms For Decision Making

  • Authors:

    Mykel J. Kochenderfer, Tim A. Wheeler, Kyle H. Wray

  • Publishers:

    Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • 700 pages
  • (18)
  • This book provides a broad introduction to algorithms for decision making under uncertainty. The book covers a wide variety of topics related to decision making, introducing the underlying mathematical problem formulations and the algorithms for solving them.
  • Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction

  • Authors:

    Kevin P. Murphy

  • Publishers:

    Massachusetts Institute of Technology
  • 860 pages
  • (203)
  • This book is by Kevin Murphy, who is the author of a previous well-known book. He is also a Senior Staff Research Scientist at Google. This book covers the basics of Machine Learning from Linear Algebra to Optimization.

Featured Lectures

  • Stanford CS229: Machine Learning Full Course taught by Andrew Ng | Autumn 2018

  • Contributors:

    Andrew Ng
  • 25 hours
  • (130)
  • Led by Andrew Ng, this course provides a broad introduction to machine learning and statistical pattern recognition. Topics include supervised learning, unsupervised learning, learning theory, reinforcement learning, and applications of machine learning.
  • MIT 6.S191: Introduction to Deep Learning

  • Contributors:

    Alexander Amini
  • 7 hours
  • (140)
  • This playlist provides a beginner-friendly introduction to the core concepts of Deep Learning. You’ll explore topics such as neural networks, perceptrons, activation functions, and training models with techniques like gradient descent and backpropagation. Through clear explanations and practical examples, this series lays a strong foundation for understanding how deep learning works and prepares you for more advanced topics and applications in the field.
  • Introduction to Machine Learning — Dmitry Kobak, 2020/21

  • Contributors:

    Dmitry Kobak
  • 11 hours
  • (954)
  • The course serves as a basic introduction to machine learning and covers key concepts in regression, classification, optimization, regularization, clustering, and dimensionality reduction. The course is aimed at master students in neuroscience and other sciences. The course assumes basic familiarity with calculus, probability theory, and linear algebra (matrices).
